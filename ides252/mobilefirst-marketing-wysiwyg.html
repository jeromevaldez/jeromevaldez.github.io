Marketing Campaign Websites

Requirements

Digital requires discovery

The digital world is hidden. Unlike the physical world which is filled with messages to grab your attention, the digital world usually requires you to discover its messages via some other source first. The physical world can get in your face with campaigns via bus shelters, billboards, street marketing. Compare that to a website that a user first has to be made aware of, then has to actively visit in order to get the message. Because of this quirk of digital marketing, you are almost never dealing with a single marketing medium when you produce interactive designs - you have to consider the campaign across multiple mediums and channels.

How are users going to discover your site or product? Via another medium like TV or radio or magazine or bus ad or social media?

So what?

This is the big question - what do you want your users to do or feel after they interact with your site or product? The sharper your focus is on this answer, the better your user experiences will become. The common desired outcomes from a digital campaign are:

  • Provide logistical information
  • Encourage a donation
  • Encourage a purchase
  • Establish brand message
  • Encourage sharing and participation (via social media)
  • Encourage user submissions of content
  • Encourage registrations or sign-ups
  • Encourage contest entry

How long will the campaign last?

The nature of most marketing campaigns is that they are time-limited. Even the Roll Up The Rim campaign happens once a year. What does this mean to you? You will be forced to make choices to balance the cost of a time-limited run vs. the benefit of a large-and-usually-expensive production.

Your next assignment - a marketing campaign website

Your client wants a website created for an upcoming marketing campaign. Design it for mobile-first because the client will use email and QR codes and Instagram and Snapchat to drive users to the campaign site. The likely first encounter with the campaign site will be on a mobile device.

I will mark the assignment based on its design on a mobile device.

Here are the options:

De-commercialize Christmas

Christmas is coming and so is the frenzy to spend. Meanwhile, in some circles, there is an earnest desire to take Christmas back from commercial interests. Is it possible to get people to rethink their Christmas spending habits? Desired outcome(s):

  • reinforce Christmas traditions that don't cost money
  • get people to question how much they spend and why they spend more during the holidays

Flu Shots

Every year Health Canada tries to get the message out that getting our flu shots is a good idea. Desired outcome(s):

  • reinforce the importance of getting a flu shot each season
  • encourage people to find a local immunization clinic at which they can get their shot

BC Hydro - Turn the heat down

Winter is coming, and along with it colder temperatures. This means that we'll be turning on heaters. Design a website that encourages people to use less energy for heating this winter. Desired outcome(s):

  • reinforce the importance of saving energy
  • (optional) get people to pledge to take some energy-saving action(s)
  • offer tips of actions that people can take to save energy
