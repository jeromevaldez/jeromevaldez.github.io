Network effects

It's massive because of network effects. And if you are creating something for impact, then you can get greater impact via larger networks.

Most major brands have some form of social presence, and want to increase it.

What makes a social integration successful or unsuccessful?

Successful social campaigns:

  • Using influencers (and others that we trust/follow) as a key component of your social campaign. e.g. HP using Robert Ayala on Vine.
  • Almost always costs less for the same amount of money as traditional TV, magazine, newspaper, or radio campaigns.
  • Often emotional.
  • Cute animals, babies doing anything adorable. This is a bit toungue-in-cheek, but seriously seems to work more often than not.
  • Appeals to our built-in: altruism, vanity, FOMO.

Social failures on the other hand:

  • Not genuine
  • Nothing worth sharing
  • Cynical
  • Easily hijacked. Lessons learned by Coke, Nutella, the NYPD, even ISIS
